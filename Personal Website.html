Two majors, one direction

I'm finishing dual B.S. degrees at RPI — Applied Physics and Computer Science, with a minor in Economics. The combination is deliberate: the questions I care about live where quantum computation meets messy, high-dimensional real-world systems, and financial markets are exactly that kind of system.

Right now I work in the X-Meng Research Group, using Qiskit to model limit-order-book dynamics in high-frequency markets. Before that: a semester at KAIST through the IIE Global E3 exchange, two summers earning IBM's Qiskit Global Summer School Quantum Excellence badge, and two semesters teaching hands-on physics labs to a class of ~30 as an undergraduate TA.

Outside the lab, I contributed to the unveiling of the IBM Quantum System One at RPI — the first on a university campus — through the Quantum Computing Club, and spent over two years in Korean Student Association leadership, where I launched a peer-mentoring program for incoming students. I like building things that help people find their footing.

featured · undergraduate research

Quantum Modeling of Limit Order Book Dynamics

X-Meng Research Group · RPI · advised by Prof. Xiangyi Meng · Dec 2025 — present

  • Encoding limit-order-book liquidity distributions with quantum-inspired binary encodings, preserving cross-sectional structure across bid–ask levels instead of collapsing the market to a single price.
  • Tracking order-book functionals — liquidity imbalance, depth concentration, liquidity pressure — to study short-term price formation and order-flow behavior.
  • Simulating and validating the models against real market data, with RPI's on-campus IBM Eagle processor as the target platform; results feed a broader CRAFT proposal and a future paper targeting PRX Quantum.
